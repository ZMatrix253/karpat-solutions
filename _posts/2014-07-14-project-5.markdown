  <div style="text-align: left;">
  Led the design and optimization of a pop-can crusher through reverse engineering, utilizing CAD and CAM software to enhance functionality, assembly, and machining processes while adhering to ASME standards.<br><br>

  <strong>Key contributions included:</strong>
  <ul>
    <li>Conducted systematic visual inspections, precise measurements, and disassembly of the pop-can crusher to gain a comprehensive understanding of the existing design.</li>
    <li>Utilized CAD modeling to create accurate 3D representations, considering functionality, assembly, machining processes, and fit requirements for improved design reliability.</li>
    <li>Maintained consistency in part dimensions using the ASME Y14.5M-1994 standard, enhancing ease of assembly and overall product quality.</li>
    <li>Employed CAM software to import CAD models, define machining operations, generate tool paths, and optimize processes for accuracy and adherence to design specifications.</li>
  </ul>  
  <strong>Outcome:</strong> Developed an optimized pop-can crusher design with enhanced manufacturing efficiency, precision, and reliability, facilitating improved production processes and potential cost reductions in fabrication.
